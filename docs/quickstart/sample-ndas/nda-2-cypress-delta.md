# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is entered into as of February 3, 2026 (the "**Effective Date**"), by and between **Cypress Holdings, LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company ("**Cypress**"), and **Delta Systems Corp.**, a Nevada corporation ("**Delta**"). Cypress and Delta are sometimes referred to herein individually as a "**Party**" and collectively as the "**Parties**."

## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to evaluate a potential commercial transaction (the "**Purpose**"). In connection therewith, each Party may exchange certain Confidential Information with the other under the terms set forth in this Agreement.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"**Confidential Information**" means information that is (a) disclosed by a Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**") in tangible form (including in writing or electronically) and is conspicuously marked or labeled as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar designation at the time of disclosure, or (b) disclosed orally and identified as confidential at the time of oral disclosure, provided that the Disclosing Party reduces such oral disclosure to a writing marked "Confidential" and delivers such writing to the Receiving Party within thirty (30) days of the oral disclosure.

Confidential Information does **not** include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was already in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure, free of any obligation of confidence; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Confidential Information; or (d) is lawfully obtained from a third party without restriction.

## 3. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall: (a) maintain the Confidential Information in confidence using a standard of care **consistent with industry practice for information of similar sensitivity**; (b) use the Confidential Information only for the Purpose; and (c) not disclose, copy, distribute, or otherwise make available the Confidential Information to any person other than as permitted herein.

## 4. Permitted Disclosures

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information solely to its **employees** who have a legitimate need to know the information in furtherance of the Purpose and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those set forth in this Agreement. The Receiving Party shall be liable for any unauthorized disclosure or use by such employees.

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent legally compelled by a court of competent jurisdiction or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party (where lawful) and reasonably cooperates with any efforts by the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order.

## 5. Term and Survival

This Agreement shall continue in effect for a period of **two (2) years** from the Effective Date. The obligations of confidentiality and non-use shall continue for the full term and shall expire upon expiration of this Agreement. No separate or extended survival period applies to information designated as trade secrets; the same two-year period governs all Confidential Information.

## 6. Return or Destruction

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party's written request at any time, the Receiving Party shall promptly destroy all Confidential Information in its possession, including any copies, summaries, or extracts thereof, and shall provide written certification of such destruction to the Disclosing Party.

## 7. Remedies

The Receiving Party acknowledges that the disclosure or unauthorized use of Confidential Information would cause irreparable harm and that monetary damages alone would be insufficient. The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 8. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws provisions. Any dispute shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in New Castle County, Delaware, and each Party submits to the jurisdiction of such courts.

## 9. Miscellaneous

No License or Other Rights. This Agreement does not grant any license under any patent, copyright, trademark, or other intellectual property right.

Entire Agreement. This Agreement is the entire agreement of the Parties as to its subject matter and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous understandings. No modification is effective unless in a writing signed by both Parties.

Severability. If any provision is held to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force.
