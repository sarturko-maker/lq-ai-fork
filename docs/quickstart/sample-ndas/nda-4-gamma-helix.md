# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is dated April 7, 2026 (the "**Effective Date**") and entered into by **Gamma Ventures, LP**, a Delaware limited partnership ("**Gamma**"), and **Helix Therapeutics, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation ("**Helix**"). Each is a "**Party**" and they are collectively the "**Parties**."

## 1. Purpose

The Parties intend to evaluate a short-term, time-bounded financing discussion (the "**Purpose**"). To facilitate the Purpose, each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other under the strict terms of this Agreement.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"**Confidential Information**" means all non-public information disclosed by a Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**"), whether disclosed orally, visually, in writing, electronically, or in any other manner, and whether or not marked or designated as confidential. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business strategies, financial projections, capitalization tables, term sheets, technical and scientific data, clinical-trial information, manufacturing processes, and customer or investor information.

The following information is **not** Confidential Information: (a) information that is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement; (b) information independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information; (c) information lawfully obtained from a third party not bound by a duty of confidentiality; or (d) information already known to the Receiving Party as evidenced by written records predating disclosure.

## 3. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information with the **highest degree of commercial care reasonable under the circumstances**, including implementation of physical, electronic, and administrative safeguards consistent with industry-leading practices. The Receiving Party shall use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and shall not commercially exploit it in any manner.

## 4. Permitted Disclosures

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its **employees** with a strict need to know for the Purpose, each of whom shall be bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those imposed by this Agreement. No disclosure is permitted to advisors, contractors, affiliates, or any other third party absent the Disclosing Party's prior written consent.

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by court order or applicable law, provided that the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) and cooperates with the Disclosing Party's efforts to limit such disclosure.

## 5. Term and Survival

This Agreement shall be effective for a period of **one (1) year** from the Effective Date. Notwithstanding such expiration, the Receiving Party's obligations with respect to Confidential Information shall continue (a) for a period of **three (3) years** following expiration or termination with respect to information that constitutes trade secrets under applicable law, and (b) for the duration of the term with respect to all other Confidential Information.

## 6. Return or Destruction

Promptly upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or earlier at the Disclosing Party's written request, the Receiving Party shall return all originals and copies of Confidential Information to the Disclosing Party, or, at the Disclosing Party's election, certify in writing the destruction of such Confidential Information.

## 7. Remedies

The Receiving Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable injury to the Disclosing Party and that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, without bond, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 8. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed under the laws of the State of Delaware, without giving effect to its conflict-of-laws rules. The Parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction in the Delaware Court of Chancery (or, if such court lacks subject-matter jurisdiction, the federal courts located in Delaware).

## 9. Miscellaneous

No License. Nothing herein grants the Receiving Party any rights, by license or otherwise, in or to any intellectual property of the Disclosing Party.

Entire Agreement. This Agreement is the complete agreement of the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof. It may be amended only by a writing signed by both Parties.

Counterparts; Electronic Signatures. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts (including by electronic signature), each of which shall be deemed an original.
