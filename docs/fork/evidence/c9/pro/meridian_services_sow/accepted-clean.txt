PROFESSIONAL SERVICES AGREEMENT
This Professional Services Agreement is entered into between Meridian Consulting Group LLC (the "Supplier") and the customer identified in the Statement of Work (the "Customer"), under which the Supplier will provide the consulting services and deliverables described in each Statement of Work.
1. Services
The Supplier shall provide the services described in each Statement of Work on a time-and-materials basis. Any timelines, estimates or staffing levels are indicative only and do not bind the Supplier.
2. Acceptance
Each deliverable shall be deemed accepted unless the Customer gives written notice of rejection within fifteen (15) business days of delivery. The Customer shall pay only for accepted deliverables, and the Supplier shall correct any rejected deliverable at its own cost and resubmit it for acceptance within ten (10) business days.
3. Fees and Expenses
The Customer shall pay all fees on a time-and-materials basis as estimated in the applicable Statement of Work, which estimate the Supplier shall not materially exceed without the Customer's prior written approval, together with all expenses at cost, within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. The Supplier may revise its rates once per year on not less than sixty (60) days' prior written notice, and any increase shall not exceed five percent (5%) per annum. Any rate revision shall apply only to Statements of Work entered into after the effective date of the revision. All fees are non-refundable.
4. Intellectual Property
All intellectual property rights in deliverables and work product created specifically for the Customer under this Agreement shall vest exclusively in the Customer upon full payment. The Supplier retains ownership of its pre-existing methodologies, tools and materials. To the extent any Supplier pre-existing intellectual property is incorporated into a deliverable, the Supplier grants the Customer a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, non-exclusive, transferable licence to use such pre-existing materials as incorporated in the deliverable for any business purpose.
5. Warranties
The Supplier warrants that the services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and that each deliverable will materially conform to the specifications in the applicable Statement of Work. The Supplier does not warrant that any deliverable will be fit for any particular purpose, and disclaims all other warranties to the fullest extent permitted by law. If a deliverable fails to conform to the foregoing warranty, the Supplier shall, at its own cost, re-perform the non-conforming services or correct the non-conforming deliverable within a reasonable time.
6. Personnel
The Supplier shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain continuity of key personnel identified in a Statement of Work. The Supplier may substitute personnel with the Customer's prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld), provided the replacement has substantially equivalent qualifications and experience.
7. Indemnity
Each party shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless the other party and its personnel against any third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities and expenses arising from: (a) in the case of Customer, the Customer's use of the deliverables in breach of this Agreement; and (b) in the case of Supplier, (i) any claim that a deliverable infringes a third party's intellectual property rights, and (ii) the Supplier's gross negligence or willful misconduct.
8. Limitation of Liability
The Supplier's aggregate liability arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed the fees paid by the Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. In no event shall either party be liable for any indirect, incidental or consequential damages, or for any loss of profits, revenue or data. The limitations and exclusions in this Section 8 shall not apply to: (a) a party's indemnification obligations under Section 7; (b) breach of confidentiality; (c) infringement or misappropriation of intellectual property rights; or (d) a party's gross negligence or willful misconduct.
9. Term and Termination
This Agreement continues until each Statement of Work is completed. Either party may terminate for convenience at any time on thirty (30) days' written notice; the other party may also terminate for convenience on the same terms. On termination the Customer shall pay all fees for work performed through the effective date of termination.
10. Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Illinois, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Illinois.
