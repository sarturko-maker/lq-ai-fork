# C-CLIENT live verification — injected company/client memory (DeepSeek, alias `smart`)

Slice **C-CLIENT** (ADR-F030): the operator's `OrganizationProfile.content_md` is injected, read-only, at the
composition seam as a fenced **"Client / house context"** block, before the area profile. This is the
**company/client tier** of the 4-level memory model. We proved the wiring *changes behaviour* with a worked
client — a synthetic in-house-legal **house context for Zendesk** (`zendesk-org-profile.md`, illustrative, not
Zendesk's real positions) — and an **A/B**: the *same* Commercial matter (a Zendesk-as-Provider MSA) and the
*same* prompt, run with the profile **OFF then ON** on the live gateway (DeepSeek; MiniMax-M3 out of quota).

Raw receipts + full answers: `ab-report.md` / `ab-report.json` (regenerated by the provider test
`tests/agents/scenarios/test_commercial_client_context.py`).

## The headline A/B — "Northwind deletes our liability cap; what's our position?"

Both legs are grounded and honest; the difference is **whose posture the answer carries**.

| | **profile OFF** | **profile ON** |
|---|---|---|
| Steps | 12 (spun: 4 search/read) | 7 |
| Framing | "a standard, mutual, **pro-Provider** cap … here is the position" — generic contract analysis | "**Per our house posture, uncapped liability of Zendesk is a hard no** … **requires escalation to the General Counsel**" |
| House-specific signals | — | **General Counsel**, **2x annual** super-cap, escalate-with-a-counter-first |
| Next step | general guidance | "we don't escalate an open question — we escalate with a clear recommendation and fallback" → drafts the counter restoring § 7 |

The discriminating signals — **"General Counsel"** and the **"2x annual fees"** super-cap rule — appear in the
ON leg **only**. They are nowhere in the matter document; they can come only from the injected house context.
(The coarse token scan also flagged "escalate"/"hard no" in the OFF answer — generic legal phrasing — which is
exactly why the *qualitative* differentiators above, not the raw counts, are the proof.) The ON leg even
mirrors the house **style** rule "give the deal owner a fallback, not just a no" by preparing the counter
*before* escalating.

## The posture flip — "we're buying SecureScan; what do we push back on?" (profile ON)

Same matter, but the prompt puts Zendesk on the **buy** side. With the house context the agent **flipped**
without being told to:

> "This is a **procurement** where Zendesk is the customer buying a tool that will process our customers'
> personal data. Two issues block signature. **1. Missing Data Processing Addendum — Blocker** … *'If we are
> buying a tool that will touch our or our customers' personal data and there are no data-processing terms,
> that blocks signature — require a DPA.'* … **2. Liability cap at three months' fees** …"

It recognised which side it was on (supplier ↔ customer), quoted the house DPA rule, and treated the missing
DPA as a hard blocker — the "flip the posture" + "DPA is mandatory" instructions working exactly as written.

## Why this is the slice working

The company/client tier is now a real input to the run: the agent acts **for** the operator's organisation —
its risk posture (cap ceiling, uncapped = GC escalation), its house style (counter-first, give a fallback),
and its which-side awareness (supplier vs procurement) — none of which is in the matter document. The block is
**read-only** (no tool mutates it; the operator owns edits via `PUT /organization-profile`) and **degrades
cleanly** when unset (the OFF leg is exactly the pre-C-CLIENT behaviour). The CI tests
(`test_agent_composition.py`) lock the mechanism: fenced + ordered-before-area + read-only + e2e
seeded-injection + empty-degrades.
